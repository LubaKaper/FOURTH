# Fourth — Account Intelligence for Maternal Health GTM

## What it is
Hospitals measure maternal success at delivery. Fourth measures what happens after — and finds the organizations whose commitments don't survive discharge.

Fourth is account intelligence for maternal health GTM teams. It scans public CMS hospital data daily, finds hospitals where CMS Birthing-Friendly commitments do not match postpartum outcome scores, calculates a 0-100 Gap Score, ranks the top 10 accounts, and generates three personalized outbound email angles for the GTM Engineer to review and send.

## The market gap
Tools like Clay, Apollo, and ZoomInfo solve general lead enrichment. Nobody has built the maternal health-specific version that understands CMS quality scores, postpartum continuity gaps, hospital commitment language, and the baby-vs-mother contrast.

The core hook: "The system you built works — for babies." Well-baby visit completion rates at the same hospital can dramatically outperform postpartum maternal visit rates, showing where infant follow-up systems are working while maternal postpartum follow-up breaks down.

## Who pays for this
Babyscripts is the target customer, along with companies like Delfina, Wildflower Health, Mahmee, and Bloomlife. Their GTM Engineer is the end user.

Hospitals are the sales targets receiving outbound emails, specifically CMOs and VP Patient Experience roles at hospitals with CMS Birthing-Friendly designation and lagging postpartum outcome scores.

The email offer is Babyscripts' remote postpartum monitoring service: BP monitoring kit, mobile app, OB-specialized care managers, and RPM CPT billing support.

Proof point: "Hospitals using Babyscripts saw patients become 2x more likely to complete their 30-day postpartum visit." (LCMC Health case study)
